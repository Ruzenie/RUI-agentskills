# Style Directions

Use these as starting points. Always adapt to product context.

## 1) Editorial Minimal
- Mood: calm, premium, intelligent.
- Traits: large typography, restrained palette, strong whitespace.
- Best for: portfolios, B2B marketing pages, knowledge products.

## 2) Data Clarity
- Mood: precise, trustworthy, analytical.
- Traits: high legibility, low decorative noise, chart-friendly neutrals.
- Best for: dashboards, admin tools, analytics SaaS.

## 3) Friendly Utility
- Mood: warm, practical, approachable.
- Traits: rounded shapes, soft contrast, supportive microcopy.
- Best for: SMB tools, productivity apps, onboarding-heavy flows.

## 4) Bold Productive
- Mood: energetic, action-oriented, modern.
- Traits: assertive accents, clear CTA hierarchy, tighter density.
- Best for: creator tools, growth products, mobile-first apps.

## 5) Neo Brutal Light
- Mood: loud, playful, memorable.
- Traits: hard edges, heavy borders, stark contrast blocks.
- Best for: campaign pages, brand-forward consumer products.

## 6) Calm Glass
- Mood: futuristic, soft, atmospheric.
- Traits: translucent layers, subtle blur, depth through surfaces.
- Best for: media products, AI assistants, premium consumer apps.

## Direction Selection Heuristic

Select direction by this order:
1. Readability and task clarity requirements.
2. Brand adjectives and target emotion.
3. Content type dominance (text/data/media/forms).
4. Platform constraints (desktop-first/mobile-first).
